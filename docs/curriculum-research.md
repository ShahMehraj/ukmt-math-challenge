## 1. Competition format

| Feature | IMC | Hamilton (IMOK Olympiad) |
|---|---|---|
| Audience | Year 11 and below | Year 10 (Cayley = Y9-, Hamilton = Y10, Maclaurin = Y11) |
| Qualification | Open | Top ~500 IMC scorers per year group / discretionary |
| Duration | 60 min | 120 min |
| Questions | 25 multiple-choice (A–E) | 6 problems, full written proofs |
| Calculator | No | No |
| Difficulty | Q1–15 accessible, Q16–20 harder, Q21–25 olympiad | All olympiad, Q1→Q6 broadly harder |
| Scoring | Q1–15: 5 marks, no penalty. Q16–20: 6 marks, **−1 wrong**. Q21–25: 6 marks, **−2 wrong**. Blank = 0. **Max 135.** | Each 0–10. **Max 60.** ~30 = Distinction. |
| Philosophy | Speed, estimation, elimination; no working shown | Rigour & communication; unjustified answers earn ~0 |

## 2. Chapter map (minimal complete coverage)

F = Foundation, I = Intermediate/IMC-core, O = Olympiad/Hamilton.

1. Number & Arithmetic Fluency (F→I, ~6h) — order of operations, signed numbers, mental-math/factoring tricks, estimation, units. *Both.*
2. Fractions, Ratio, Proportion (F→I, ~7h). *Both.*
3. Percentages (I, ~5h) — change, reverse %, successive %, "x% more vs y% less". *IMC.*
4. Number Theory (I→O, ~12h) — primes, factorisation, divisor counting, HCF/LCM, divisibility, modular/remainder, parity, terminating decimals, factorial units digits. *Both.*
5. Algebraic Manipulation (I→O, ~12h) — expand/factor, linear & simultaneous, quadratics, identities, digit/place-value algebra. *Both.*
6. Sequences & Patterns (I→O, ~6h). *Both.*
7. Indices, Powers & Surds (I, ~5h). *IMC.*
8. Angles & Polygons (F→I, ~8h). *Both.*
9. Triangles, Pythagoras & Similarity (I→O, ~10h) — similar triangles & ratio chaining. *Both.*
10. Circles (I→O, ~7h). *Both.*
11. Area, Perimeter & Composite Shapes (I→O, ~8h) — shaded = big − pieces. *Both.*
12. Coordinate Geometry & Transformations (I, ~5h). *IMC.*
13. 3D Geometry & Mensuration (I→O, ~6h) — nets, plane cuts, F/E/V counts. *Both.*
14. Combinatorics & Counting (I→O, ~10h). *Both.*
15. Probability (I, ~4h). *IMC.*
16. Logic, Reasoning & Word Problems (I→O, ~8h) — liars, deduction, speed-distance-time, rate/work. *Both.*
17. Olympiad Proof Techniques (O, ~14h) — contradiction, construction + lower bound, "find all" completeness, pigeonhole, invariants, bounded casework, writing rigorous solutions. **Hamilton capstone.**

Total ≈ 133h (~60h to IMC mastery; remainder deepens to Hamilton level).
Early parallel tracks: 1→2→3 and 8→9. Spine: 4,5,6,7. Capstone 17 after 4,5,9,14 solid.

## 3. Recurring archetypes

**IMC:** compute-avoidance arithmetic (factor first); awkward-denominator fractions;
percentage change & reverse %; primes-in-range / factorial-units / divisor counting;
linear systems as word problems (add the equations); composite shaded area; Pythagoras
with surds; angle-chase quadrilateral classification; coordinate transformation chains;
cube-cut / net / polyhedron counts; consecutive-integer means. Q21–25 layer two ideas.

**Hamilton:** "find all n…" (completeness proof); "find min/max and prove it"
(construction + lower bound); "show impossible / find the error" (contradiction);
digit/PIN place-value; polygon-angle → quadratic; similar-triangle ratio chaining;
inscribed-circle radius; tiling/Diophantine optimisation; residue-class counting;
process/invariant problems; algebraic-identity construction (sum of squares).

## 4. Common traps

**IMC:** guessing penalty on Q16–25 (blank beats a wrong guess); "x% more ≠ x% less";
saving-per-item vs total; engineered distractors matching specific slips; surd/radius-vs-diameter
carelessness; over-classifying shapes; 3D off-by-one (one cut adds one face); unit/magnitude slips.

**Hamilton:** stating without proving; incomplete "find all" (must bound the search);
half a min/max proof; disproof-by-example instead of contradiction; geometry without named
angle reasons; unbounded casework.

## 5. Foundations brush-up (before Chapter 1)

Whole-number arithmetic & BIDMAS; fractions/decimals/percentages & conversions; times
tables & factors; pre-algebra (substitute, solve 1–2 step linear, expand a bracket); basic
angle facts (180°/360°/triangle), shape names, rectangle/triangle area & perimeter; metric
units; coordinates in four quadrants; careful problem reading. For Hamilton: writing in full
sentences, meaning of "prove/show that/find all", and parity reasoning.
